JAPAN

Japan (日本) is an island country of East Asia in the northwest Pacific Ocean. It borders the Sea of Japan to the west and extends from the Sea of Okhotsk in the north to the East China Sea and Taiwan in the south. Discover the Land of the Rising Sun.

What's Popular?

Mount Fuji, located on the Honshu island of Japan, is the highest mountain in the country, with a beautiful symmetrical cone shape that has made it a prominent symbol of Japan and an iconic destination for hikers and tourists alike.

Mount Fuji

Osaka Castle, a historic landmark in Japan, is a magnificent fortress that played a significant role in the unification of Japan during the sixteenth century, and is now a popular tourist attraction showcasing the country's rich cultural heritage.

Osaka Castle

Tempozan Giant Ferris Wheel, The Tempozan Giant Ferris Wheel is a popular tourist attraction located in Osaka, Japan, offering stunning panoramic views of the city and its surroundings from a height of 112.5 meters.

Tempozan Giant Ferris Wheel

Inokashira Park, located in the western part of Tokyo, is a spacious park with a large pond, beautiful cherry blossom trees, and a peaceful atmosphere that makes it a popular spot for picnics, strolls, and boating, especially during the spring season.

Inokashira Park

Tokyo National Museum,the oldest and largest museum in Japan, housing an extensive collection of over 110,000 art objects and historical artifacts, including samurai swords, Buddhist sculptures, ceramics, textiles, and ancient manuscripts.

Tokyo National Museum

Asakusa, is a traditional district in Tokyo that is famous for its iconic Senso-ji Temple, a bustling shopping street filled with traditional snacks and souvenirs, and a lively atmosphere that captures the essence of old Tokyo.

Asakusa

Fukuoka Castle, also known as Maizuru Castle, is a historical landmark located in Fukuoka City, Japan, that was originally built in the 17th century and served as the residence of the Kuroda family, one of the most powerful feudal clans of the time.

Fukuoka Castle

Kyoto Imperial Palace, located in the heart of Kyoto, Japan, was the residence of the Imperial family until 1868, and now serves as a popular tourist destination for those looking to explore the rich cultural and historical heritage of Japan.

Kyoto Imperial Palace
